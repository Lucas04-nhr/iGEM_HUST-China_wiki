Section 1 : Introduction

Our project aimed to enable Cyanobacteria to produce lactic acid at a high efficiency, enhance the electricity producing capacity of Shewanella, as well as improve the resistance of both bacteria. In order to achieve these goals, we construct mainly 4 plasmids: pLactate and pResistence6803 for Cyanobacteria, pElectricity and pResistance MR-1 for Shewanella. Besides 4 main plasmids, we also constructed 3 plasmids in order to test the function of genes more conveniently.

We transferred them into Synechocystis PCC6803 and Shewallena MR-1 for expressing the proteins we needed. At the same time, in order to verify the efficiency of electricity production, we also carried out and verified our project from all aspects.

Section 2 : Plasmid amplification and reconstruction

We first transformed the plasmids containing designed sequence synthesized by the company into Escherichia coli Top10 for amplification, so we have enough amount of plasmid to carry out subsequent experiments. Then colony PCR showed a successful transformation, and required plasmids were extracted for the next experiment.

We then obtained target fragments by means of PCR, and then constructed them into the plasmids we need.

Section 4 : Expression Validation

Synechocystis PCC6803

4.1.1 Lactate production verification

We co-culture Synechocystis PCC6803 and S.oneidensis MR-1. The nutrient of S.oneidensis MR-1 is lactate produced by Synechocystis PCC6803. We tried to determine the production of lactate by HPLC to test the feasibility of the co-culture method. We first measured the standard curve of lactate concentration and determined its retention time. The Synechocystis PCC6803(pLactate) cells were broken by freeze-thaw method, and the supernatant after centrifugation was taken for high performance liquid chromatography (HPLC). A peak with almost the same retention time which is 3.912 min as the lactate standard substance was observed on the spectrum, which confirmed the successful expression of lactic acid in engineered bacteria. The peak area was compared with the standard curve. It can be seen that the lactic acid yield of our engineering bacteria cyanobacteria was (see figure), indicating that the pLactate gene contained in the plactate gene could produce lactic acid stably and provide nutrition for Shivaella stably. This result indicated the successful expression of the imported LdhA and LldP genes, and verified the feasibility of the co-culture system.

1) testing the micro battery

Using the micro battery, we use a digital multimeter to measure the output voltage of microbial fuel cell. We measured the voltage of the ycel-pncB, NAD-Nadd-NADM gene pathway in Shiwanella and compared it with the voltage of wild-type Shiwanella. Measure a set of voltages every three hours and compare as follows. The results showed that the voltage generated by the gene pathway containing ycel-pncB and NAD-Nadd-NADM in the logarithmic growth stage of Shiwanella was significantly higher than that of wild-type Shiwanella, which verified that we successfully introduced the target gene and its normal expression and function in Shiwanella.

2) Photosensitizer & riboflavin

In our search for relevant papers, we learned that photosensitivities and riboflavin contribute to extracellular electron transfer, thereby improving the efficiency of electricity generation. Therefore, we investigated the effect of addition of photosensitizers (eosin Y, fluorescein) and riboflavin on electricity production in Shiwanella. We compared the electrical production of the experimental group supplemented with photosensitizers (eosin Y, fluorescein) and riboflavin with the control group. It was found that when photosensitizers (eosin Y, fluorescein) and riboflavin were added, the experimental group with riboflavin was significantly higher than the control group. The results show that these additives are helpful in generating electricity, and considering the low cost of riboflavin, they can be applied in subsequent industrial production.

3) Practical applicatio

In order to verify the practical application effect of the microbattery, we connected the microbattery containing the gene pathways of ycel-pncB, NAD-Nadd-NADM and wild-type microbattery with the red LED lamp bead in series on a pathway, respectively. The results showed that the brightness of LED beads in the wild-type group did not change significantly after being connected with the battery, indicating that the wild-type micro battery did not reach the rated operating voltage of LED beads, while the red LED beads in the engineering bacteria group could be used normally and emit bright red light. The results showed that the gene we transferred significantly improved the electricity generation capacity of Shiwanella. And can actually provide electrical energy for some electrical appliances.

Co-culture system

4.3.1 Co-culture growth

We attempted to combine the BG11 medium required for cyanobacteria culture with the M9 buffer used by Shiwanella to generate electricity and configured MBG11 medium to achieve the growth of two kinds of bacteria through the same medium, so as to prepare for the subsequent continuous culture experiment.

We used MBG11 culture medium to culture cystidia and Shiwanella respectively, and measured OD values of cystidia culture medium at 730nm and Shiwanella culture medium at 600nm. The OD value is measured every 24 hours, and a growth curve is obtained as follows. It can be seen that MBG11 culture can make the normal growth of cystidia. However, at the same time, we found that Shewanella could grow at present, but the growth rate was slow. When the bacteria reached the stable stage, the concentration of the bacteria was not high, and the growth situation was far less than that cultured in LB medium. We analyzed that the reason for this result might be the lack of nutrients. At the same time, we also noticed that when M9 buffer was used to generate electricity for a long time, the bacterial concentration in the device was almost unchanged, indicating that in a low-nutrient environment, Shewanella could only maintain survival and generate electricity but could not grow and multiply. In response to this situation. We will further improve the medium system in the future, and at the same time increase the concentration of Shewanella bacteria in LB medium in advance and add MBG11 medium to produce electricity.
